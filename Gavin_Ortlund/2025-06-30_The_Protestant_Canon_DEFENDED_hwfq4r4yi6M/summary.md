# Summary

Gavin Ortlund hosts Ben (Cleave to Antiquity) and Javier Purdomo for a roundtable on the **Protestant canon** of Scripture. They argue that common Catholic and Orthodox apologetic lines—“**fallible list of infallible books**,” “no canon until the Reformation,” **Council of Rome** as proof of the 73-book Bible—overstate Protestant instability and understate **ambiguity inside other traditions**. The discussion separates **infallibility** from **binding authority**, surveys **diverse canons and Trent readings**, defends a **historical two-tier** treatment of Old Testament books (fully canonical vs. ecclesiastical/edifying) in patristic and classical Protestant sources, and closes with a **positive** appeal: follow the Old Testament **Jesus and the apostles** presupposed—supported by **Josephus**, early lists such as **Melito** (via **Eusebius**), and careful handling of **Jerome** and medieval **Glossa** material. A pastoral coda redirects existential anxiety toward **Christ** and ordinary spiritual discipline.

# Core Question

Can Protestants **know** which books are Scripture without an **infallible** church list—and is their position uniquely **unstable** compared to Rome, the East, and the **first millennium**?

# Key Concepts

- **Canon** and **sola scriptura** as a standard debate flashpoint
- **Infallibility vs. authority** (umpires, courts, excommunication)
- **66 / 73 / variable Orthodox** Bibles; **Trent** read as **closing** vs. **baseline** canon
- **Two-tier canon**: narrow **canon list** vs. **citation** of other books as “Scripture” for edification
- **Jerome**, **Gelasian/Council of Rome** manuscript debates, **Glossa Ordinaria**
- **Josephus** (*Against Apion* 1.8), **Melito of Sardis** / **Eusebius**, **Roger Beckwith** on Melito’s numbering
- **Pastoral**: certainty ultimately in **Christ**, not only technical winning

# Why It Matters

The canon is where **epistemology**, **church history**, and **devotional fear** meet. Viewers wrestling with conversion, apologetics, or doubt need both **clear arguments** (so objections are not inflated into defeaters) and **honest history** (so Protestantism is not caricatured—or defended—as something it never was). The episode also models **charitable** use of Catholic encyclopedias and Catholic scholars (**Michuta**) while still pressing **methodological** critiques.
