## Detailed Outline

### Setting and tone (September 11 context)

- **Father Josiah** (Patristic Nectar) hosts **Dr. Gavin Ortlund** on the **24th anniversary of 9/11**, remembering those killed and praying for families.
- The conversation also occurs shortly after the murder of **Charlie Kirk** (Turning Point USA); both speakers acknowledge **grief**, **evil abroad**, and the value of **standing together** in a dark time.
- Ortlund calls the interview a **bright point** in his day; both express **mutual respect** and desire to **build relationship** despite differences.

### Family and California connections

- Father Josiah studied at **Westmont College** (mid‑1980s) with **Nels Ortlund** (Gavin’s uncle).
- Josiah’s parents attended **Lake Avenue Congregational Church** when **Ray Ortlund Sr.** was pastor.
- Gavin clarifies lineage: **Ray Sr.** (grandfather), **Ray Jr.** (father), uncles and siblings including **Christa**, **Eric** (Anglican teacher in London), **Dane** (Presbyterian minister)—a family joke: Baptist, Presbyterian, Anglican “at the bar” together.
- Gavin lived in **Southern California** about **15 years** (near **Pasadena** / **Sierra Madre**); he and his wife miss California and plan a visit for the **marriage conference** (parents watching **five children**).

### Gavin Ortlund: faith, education, and current roles

- **Conversion** around age **10**; faith deepened in high school via **First Presbyterian Church of Augusta, Georgia** (youth ministry, worship, teaching).
- Vocation tension: **many relatives in ministry**—he worked through whether his call was genuine or merely **familial imitation**; concluded family is neither a reason to enter nor to avoid ministry—**follow Jesus**.
- **Undergraduate:** University of Georgia, **philosophy and religion** (early love: **Kierkegaard**, existentialism).
- **M.Div.:** **Covenant Seminary** (Presbyterian), pastoral internship, then long ministry in **Southern California**; ~**1.5 years** before recording in **Tennessee**.
- **Ph.D.:** **Fuller Seminary**, **historical theology**; dissertation on **Anselm of Canterbury** and the **Proslogion** (including the **ontological argument**); loves **Augustine**, **church fathers**, **Puritans**, recent theologians; publishes mainly historical theology.
- **Doctoral supervisor:** **Oliver Crisp**; also worked with **John Thompson** and others at Fuller.
- **Ordination:** **2013** at **Sierra Madre Congregational Church**.
- **Current service:** **theologian in residence** at **Emmanuel Church, Nashville**—Sunday teaching, Wednesday seminar; **visiting professor** at **Phoenix Seminary** (periodic flights).

### Truth Unites: origin, buckets, and naming

- Online ministry **surprised** him; **pandemic‑era YouTube** watching (debates, e.g. **William Lane Craig**) intersected with finishing a book on **God’s existence**.
- Motivation: reach people—especially **young men**—who won’t come to church or read books but **use YouTube** for questions (“catechized” by YouTube/podcasts).
- **Vision:** **gospel assurance**—each video should shepherd viewers toward **forgiveness**, **hope**, **God’s love**, **assurance**; still go **deep** (theology, church history) because people want **substance**.
- **Rough “buckets” of content:** (1) **general apologetics**; (2) **Protestant apologetics**; (3) **triaging** issues (e.g. **creation**—what should be **first‑rank** vs not); (4) **general theology education**; (5) **pastoral** videos—plus examples like **evangelistic** (Big Bang / Joe Rogan) vs **expository** (e.g. **Christ’s descent**, **Apostles’/Athanasian Creeds**, patristic consensus).
- **Name “Truth Unites”:** from **John 17:11**—in a time of **combativeness and fracturing**, he wants to be a **constructive** voice; **truth divides and unites**; in polemics he aims for **openhearted** engagement and **common ground**, not only disagreement.

### Theology: Reformed Baptist, tradition, and Craig

- **High esteem** for **William Lane Craig** but **differences**—they discussed this on Gavin’s channel; Gavin doesn’t see himself as a top‑tier **debater**.
- Self‑description: **Reformed Baptist**—**1689 Second London Baptist Confession** functions somewhat like **Westminster** for Presbyterians.
- Emphasizes **respect for Christian tradition** and **historical theology** (“testimony of the ages”)—e.g. taking seriously **universal early affirmation** (to his knowledge) of themes like **Christ’s descent** referenced in creeds.
- **Ecumenical** in a **good** sense: **principled** search for **agreement**, not **papering over** differences; contrast with unhealthy ecumenism.
- **Father Josiah** took **Christian doctrine** from **Craig** senior year at **Westmont**; recalls Craig’s **middle knowledge** lectures and later **campus ministry** and debates.

### Baptist confessional recovery

- Gavin’s passion: many **contemporary Baptists** lack **confessionalism**; **17th‑century Baptist** sources show **rigor**; **Congregationalists, Baptists, Presbyterians** share **Reformed** roots but diverged—he wants Baptists **reacquainted** with **church history** and their own **roots**.

### Marriage conference: purpose, series, and Gavin’s contribution

- **Patristic Nectar** hosts **Lenten** (spiritual life) and **fall** conferences; context: reported **revival of interest** in Christianity among **young people** (YouTube‑centered), including interviews on **conversion** and **Orthodoxy**.
- **Collaboration** across **Orthodox, Catholic, Protestant** on **shared high‑level values** for the culture.
- **Five‑year fall series** (intended **annual** topics): (1) **marriage** as **bedrock of civilization**—social role of marriage/family (**not** mainly interior mystery of marriage); (2) **patriotism** (planned for **250th** anniversary of the US); (3) **motherhood**; (4) **technology**; (5) **education**—hope for **repentance** and **basic Christian morality** nationally.
- **First conference:** **Friday–Saturday, October 10–11** (year discussed: **Friday Oct 10 – Saturday Oct 11**) at **Fox Theater**, **downtown Riverside**, near **Mission Inn**; tickets: **marriageconference.com**.
- **Gavin’s aims:** marriage as **social building block**; **foundations rumbling**—**civilization** assaulted at roots including **marriage**; Christians must **distinguish** agreement vs disagreement and still be **salt and light**.
- **His talk:** **What is marriage?**—**C.S. Lewis**, especially ***That Hideous Strength*** (marriage vs modernity); **Matthew 19**—“**What God has joined together**”—marriage **before** government; **divine institution**, not mere human contract; **joy** and **fun** (he shares life with **five young kids**); push back on **hookup culture**’s lie that marriage is **confining**—**God’s commands** aim at **flourishing**.
- **Father Josiah:** marriage is also **hard**—**self‑exposure**, **fidelity**, **repentance**; cites **Chrysostom** on **Hebrews** (commentary left rough due to **exile**)—image of **two rocks** striking **fire** vs **two persons** committed in marriage (**“consecrated friendship”** in the tradition of **St. Seraphim of Sarov**); culture discards everything—marriage as **lasting gift**.
- **Brad Wilcox** (University of Virginia), author of ***Get Married*** (subtitle about **defying elites** and **saving civilization**), also speaking—**myth‑busting** about marriage and happiness.

### Protestant–Orthodox collaboration: stories and principles

- Some **critics** in each tradition dislike **cross‑traditional collaboration**; both speakers think that is **unfortunate**.
- **Father Josiah’s examples:** local **mayoral** / **environmental commission** work with diverse clergy; **Mount Rubidoux** cross (**Riverside**) threatened by **Americans United for Separation of Church and State** (~**2011–12**)—**40+ pastors** signed a **respectful appeal**; cross **preserved**; city officials **thanked** clergy—rare **public witness** of unity.
- Cross site: long‑running **Protestant Easter sunrise** service since **1907**.
- **World Congress of Families** (e.g. **Utah 2015**, **Tbilisi 2016**, **Budapest 2017**)—scholars, activists, politicians, pastors for **marriage and family**; **societal impact** of such collaboration should not be **underestimated**.
- **Gavin:** **100% agreement**—**common grace**; e.g. **pro‑life** work with **anyone** willing—would be wrong to refuse **saving lives** over **theological** differences.
- Model: **argue openly** about **differences** (they **matter**); **simultaneously** cooperate where Christians **agree**, especially for **societal witness** in **dark**, **fragmented**, **internet‑radicalized** times—support **basics** of **Christian heritage**.

## Core Ideas

- **Cross‑traditional collaboration** on **culture‑shaping goods** (marriage, family, life) is **biblically** and **practically** warranted without **denying** serious **doctrinal** differences.
- **Truth** can **unite** (John 17) even when it also **divides**; public discourse should combine **clarity** with **charity** and **historical awareness**.
- **Marriage** is a **divine**, **pre‑political** institution (**Matthew 19**); renewing its **honor** benefits **society**, especially amid moral and **civilizational** pressure.
- **Common grace** grounds **co‑belligerence** on issues like **abortion** and **religious liberty** with people who disagree on **ecclesiology** or **sacraments**.
