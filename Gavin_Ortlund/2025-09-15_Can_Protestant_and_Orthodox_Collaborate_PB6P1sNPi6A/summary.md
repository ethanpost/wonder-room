# Summary

Father Josiah (Patristic Nectar) interviews Gavin Ortlund on personal history, the **Truth Unites** YouTube ministry, Reformed Baptist theology, and an upcoming **Orthodox–Catholic–Protestant** marriage conference in Riverside. The conversation grounds **collaboration** in real stories—family ties to California churches, **common grace** and **pro-life** partnership, and a successful **multi-pastor** appeal that preserved a historic **Mount Rubidoux** cross—while insisting that **doctrinal** differences remain **openly** and **honestly** disputed.

# Core Question

How can Christians who disagree on **ecclesiology**, **sacraments**, and **confession** still **co-labor** for marriage, family, and the **public good** without pretending their divisions are trivial?

# Key Concepts

- **Gospel assurance** as the pastoral aim of Truth Unites (depth plus evangelistic warmth)
- **1689 Second London Baptist Confession** and recovery of **Baptist** **confessional** depth
- **John 17:11** and the name **Truth Unites** (truth as gathering amid fracture)
- **Marriage** as **divine institution** before the state (**Matthew 19**; **C.S. Lewis**, *That Hideous Strength*)
- **Common grace** as a basis for **cross-traditional** action (e.g. **pro-life** coalitions)
- **Civic ecumenism**: dozens of pastors uniting on a **religious liberty** / **heritage** issue with measurable **public** impact

# Why It Matters

The interview models a **practical** path between **sectarian** withdrawal and **doctrinal** **minimalism**: name **differences**, pursue **truth** in print and debate, and still **stand together** where **creation order** and **neighbor-love** require a **united** Christian **witness**—especially when **marriage** and **life** are under pressure and **secular** culture is **fragmented**.
