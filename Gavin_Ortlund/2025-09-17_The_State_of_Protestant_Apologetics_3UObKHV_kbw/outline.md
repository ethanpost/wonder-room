## Detailed Outline

### Introduction: catalyst and aim

- **Pastor Ben** of **Cleave to Antiquity** announced he is becoming **Eastern Orthodox**—significant mainly in a **small corner of the internet**.
- Ben had a **growing YouTube channel** in **Protestant apologetics**, so the move surprised many viewers.
- Ortlund’s focus here is **not** primarily to **rehash or critique Ben’s conversion** but to **look forward** and **analyze the state of Protestant apologetics** with **three points**—the **third** is the **longest**, most **substantive**, and in some ways **most surprising**.

### 1) Take a deep breath

- **Internet conversions** trigger strong **emotion**: **hurt**, **betrayal** when someone leaves “your team,” especially if they **influenced you** or did **Protestant apologetics** until shortly before converting.
- Such **concerns** can be **legitimate**; Protestants may **observe**, **argue**, and **express concern**—but **heat-of-the-moment** dynamics and **sociology** make it easy to respond **uncharitably**.
- The **reverse** also appears: **triumphalism** when someone joins one’s side—e.g. claims that **“it’s over for Protestantism.”**
- **Internet apologetics** is only **one small corner** of a **much larger** religious and cultural world—**step back** and **see the bigger picture**.
- Ortlund is **not** yet engaging **Ben’s specific arguments** in depth: he wants **more time** to hear **what** the arguments are; from Ben’s video and interview with **Father Peter Hirs** (as named in his remarks), little so far clarifies **why Eastern Orthodoxy specifically** as opposed to e.g. **Oriental Orthodoxy**. More may be **forthcoming**; until then, **pause** before full critique.

### 2) Touch grass

- **Meaning:** avoid being **sucked into the internet** in ways that raise **anxiety**, **anger**, and **distorted** perspective—Ortlund says this as something **he** must remember **daily**, not from superiority.
- Pushback on **“the internet isn’t real life”**: the internet **is** part of real life (bills, relationships, information)—but it is **not the whole** of life.
- **Hold it loosely**: **intentional** time **away**, **unplugging**, **other focuses**; at **42** with prior life experience he still struggles; for **22-year-olds** raised on YouTube it may be **harder**.
- He uses **discipline**, **systems**, and **plans**: **when to take breaks**, **when not to respond** to criticism, **when to respond**, **staying on mission**.
- **Stepping back** into **face-to-face relationships**, **nature**, **ordinary joy** shows the online storm is **smaller** than it feels—again, **one corner** of the internet vs. the **whole world**.

### 3) Protestants, speak up (substantive section)

- **Observation:** Protestants often **do not defend Protestantism** as consistently or visibly as some traditions defend their **ecclesial** claims; energy goes to **general Christian apologetics**, **culture war**, and **politics**—understandable, but online **Protestantism is under-represented and under-defended**.
- Result: **ignorance** of the **depth and rigor** in Protestant thought; a joke that Ortlund and **Wes Huff** give Protestantism a **“scholarly aesthetic”** on YouTube actually shows how **narrow** popular exposure is if **YouTube** substitutes for **reading scholarship**—**mutual ignorance** across “teams” is common.
- **Church history** examples (Protestant contributions): **Philip Schaff**—*History of the Christian Church*, **Nicene and Post-Nicene Fathers** series (still standard); **J. N. D. Kelly**, *Early Christian Doctrines*; **James Ussher** identified **authentic letters of Ignatius**; **J. B. Lightfoot** produced a definitive **critical edition of the Apostolic Fathers**; **“Prology”** as a term distinct from **Patristics** (systematic study of **all** early Christian-related texts, not only orthodox ones) comes from **17th-century Lutheran scholastics**, especially **Johann Gerhard**.
- Thesis: many people are unaware that **Protestants** are often **leading** serious **historical** and **text-critical** work—not only “allowed” to be deep in history.

### YouTube vs. scholarship: personal experience

- **Channel** began **August 2020** (COVID boredom as a **pastor**); drew on channels like **Pines with Aquinus** and **Capturing Christianity** (as named in the transcript), **debates**—initially to promote a book on **God’s existence**; he was pulled into **Protestant apologetics** by **need**.
- **Icon veneration** video generated intense **volume** of responses (dozens, some **3+ hours**); impossible to watch everything; some fault him for **not** answering every reply—**entitlement** and **swarming** dynamics.
- Some Orthodox critics call him a **liar** on icon veneration or say he ignores correction; he often **does not know which video** they mean. His **original** claims were, in his view, **standard** in **mainstream scholarship** (e.g. cites **Richard Price** as translator on **Second Council of Nicaea**); saying so publicly **ignites** controversy among viewers **unfamiliar** with that scholarly baseline.
- **Plea:** more **scholars** helping in the **YouTube** space; he frames his work partly as **missionary** impulse to **meet need**.
- **Positive examples:** **Jordan Cooper**; **Ryan Reeves** (church history); younger voices like **Shawn Luke** (Anglican Aesthetics), **Javier Perdomo**—**both/and**: you **don’t** have to be a scholar or pastor to have a channel, but the community needs **more** voices with **credentials** and **experience**.

### Social media, hunger, and who should engage

- **Tweet** (paraphrased thrust): If you are an **ordained minister** or **credentialed academic** worried about **influencers’** disproportionate influence—**speak up**. **Spiritual hunger** and **anxiety** are intense; without **good teachers/mentors**, people learn from **bad** ones. **Social media** is **forming** people—for better or worse; hunger for **spiritual fathers** and **theological mentors** is real.
- **Advice on who should dive in:** those who are **eager** should often be **more cautious**; those who are **cautious** should often be **more eager** (not purely an **age** rule).
- **Caution:** **22** and wanting **fame** is probably **not** the right motive **right now**—but **exceptions** exist (e.g. **Redeemed Zoomer**—young, skillful, helpful on denominations).
- **Primary encouragement:** people with a **long track record** of **ministry** and/or **academia**—e.g. **35-year-old pastor**, **45-year-old seminary professor**—consider a **side** YouTube presence to **mentor** younger people in these debates: **“fortress to battlefield.”**
- **Not elitist:** **Charles Spurgeon** had **no doctorate**; credentials are **not** required to **serve** God’s kingdom—but those **with** credentials are **especially** urged to **join** the work. If they **don’t**, others will—some **uncredentialed** people do **great** work.

### Outro

- He **enjoys** YouTube and needs **breaks**; **gratitude** for the work.
- **Short-term:** **Protestant apologetics** will be **somewhat less** the focus while he **writes a book** and leans toward **general Christian apologetics** (his **heart**); he will still do **some** Protestant apologetics.

## Core Ideas

- **Emotional discipline** and **charity** matter when reacting to **public conversions**—avoid **uncharitable** heat and **triumphalism**; **internet** debates are **not** the whole church or world.
- **Digital hygiene** (“touch grass”): treat the internet as **part** of life but **not** all of it; **unplug** for **relationships**, **nature**, and **perspective**.
- **Protestantism** suffers from **under-defense** online relative to other traditions, not from an absence of **serious scholarship**—many **landmark** contributions in **patristics**, **text criticism**, and **terminology** come from **Protestant** scholars.
- **Popular apologetics** (YouTube) and **academic** work are **poorly connected**; more **credentialed** voices are needed **without** despising **non-experts** who serve well.
- **Mentorship** and **teacher quality** on social media are **urgent** because **formation** happens there whether or not the church “approves.”

## Key Terms

- **Protestant apologetics** — defense of **distinctively Protestant** claims (vs. general Christian or purely cultural apologetics).
- **Internet conversions** — public faith shifts amplified by **social media**, with distinctive **emotional** and **tribal** dynamics.
- **Touch grass** — informal call to **disengage** from online intensity and **re-engage** embodied life.
- **Prology** — systematic study of **all** early texts related to Christianity (coinage tied to **17th-century Lutheran** usage in the talk), distinguished from **Patristics** (orthodox fathers).
- **Second Council of Nicaea (787)** — context for **icon** controversy; **Richard Price** mentioned as a scholarly voice in translation/discussion.
