# Summary

Gavin Ortlund uses Pastor Ben’s (Cleave to Antiquity) public move toward Eastern Orthodoxy as a **news peg**, but the video’s aim is **forward-looking**: to offer **three** responses to the **state of Protestant apologetics**—**(1)** calm down and **avoid uncharitable** or **triumphalist** online reactions; **(2)** **“touch grass,”** i.e. treat the internet as **part** of life but **not** the whole, and **disengage** enough to preserve **peace and perspective**; **(3)** **Protestants should speak up more**, because **popular online space** under-represents Protestant **depth**, while **real scholarship** (he cites several **historians and editors** by name) is often **disconnected** from **YouTube** debates. He shares **personal** experience of **volume**, **entitlement**, and **misrepresentation** around controversial videos, **names** several **helpful** Protestant channels, and **pleads** with **ordained** and **credentialed** Christians to **enter** or **support** online **mentorship**—while **rejecting crass elitism** (e.g. **Spurgeon**). He closes by noting **short-term** emphasis on **writing** and **general Christian** apologetics over **strictly Protestant** apologetics.

# Core Question

When **high-profile** online **conversions** spike **emotion** and **tribal** rhetoric, how should Protestants **respond with charity and scale**, and how can **scholarly** and **pastoral** **depth** be **better connected** to **popular** **apologetics**—especially where **social media** is **forming** the **hungry**?

# Key Concepts

- **Internet conversion dynamics** — **betrayal**, **triumphalism**, and **uncharitable** heat in a **small** online **corner** vs. the **wider** church and world.
- **Touch grass** — **digital boundaries** so the net does not **inflate** **anxiety** and **anger** or **replace** **embodied** life.
- **Under-defense of Protestantism online** — energy often flows to **generic** apologetics or **politics**, leaving **ecclesial** Protestant claims **less visible**.
- **Scholarship vs. YouTube** — **standard** academic **baselines** (e.g. on **Nicaea II** / **icon** history) can **sound radical** to viewers **only** exposed to **feeds**.
- **Mentorship gap** — **hunger** for **fathers** and **teachers** online; **credentialed** voices **needed** **without** **dismissing** **faithful** **lay** **workers**.

# Why It Matters

The talk names a **practical** problem: **formation** now happens heavily through **screens**. If **serious** Protestant **history**, **text criticism**, and **theology** stay **invisible** where **debates** rage, **mischaracterization** and **polarization** fill the **void**—and **young** people **starved** of **good** **mentors** will **learn** from **whoever** shows up. Ortlund’s argument is that **emotional discipline**, **embodied** **balance**, and **more** **qualified** **voices** in **public** **spaces** are **pastoral** responses, not mere **branding**.
