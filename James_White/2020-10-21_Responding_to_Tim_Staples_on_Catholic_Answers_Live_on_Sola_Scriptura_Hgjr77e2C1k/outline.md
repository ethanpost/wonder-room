# Responding to Tim Staples on Catholic Answers Live on Sola Scriptura

## Metadata

- Author: James White
- Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Hgjr77e2C1k
- Date: 2020-10-21
- Folder: James_White

## Detailed Outline

### Context and purpose

- James White responds to a **Catholic Answers Live** segment in which **Tim Staples** debated a caller named **Jeff** about **sola scriptura** and tradition.
- White’s aim is to show how Catholic apologetic “rapid-fire” proof-texting (he compares it to **Jerry Matatics** on Mary) can **sound** impressive until references are checked in context.
- He invites listeners to use a **Bible program** and to add **historical background** (e.g. **Mishna**, synagogue practice) rather than accepting slogans at face value.

### Caller Jeff’s opening move (Mark / Matthew)

- Jeff argues that **Mark 7** (parallel **Matthew 15**, not Matthew 13) implicitly supports **sola scriptura** by contrasting **God’s commandment** with the **tradition of the elders**.
- He identifies that tradition with what later Judaism calls **oral Torah**, documented in sources such as the **Talmud** / **Mishna**.

### Staples-style reply: “Jesus is not condemning all tradition”

- A common Catholic apologetic response: Jesus attacks **bad** or **human** traditions, not **tradition in general**.
- Examples often cited in such exchanges (as Staples does): **Matthew 23:1–3** and the **chair of Moses**; **Luke 16** and **Abraham’s bosom**; **Matthew 2:23** and supposed **oral prophecy**; **2 Thessalonians 2:15** and **3:6** (stand fast in / walk according to **tradition**).

### White’s thesis: mechanism for testing “divine” tradition

- White grants that **not every use of “tradition” in Scripture is negative**.
- His focus is **epistemology**: how do you tell **true** from **false** tradition when religious leaders claim **divine** origin?
- In **Matthew 15 / Mark 7**, the **Corban** rule is treated by the Pharisees as part of **oral Torah**—not merely as a casually “human” custom.
- White argues Jesus’s rebuttal **invalidates their tradition by appeal to the written word of God** (“you invalidated the word of God for the sake of your tradition”), establishing **Scripture as the higher test** for claims about divine tradition.

### Background: Mishna, Aboth, and the Corban rule

- White distinguishes **Talmud** from being precise: **tractate Aboth** in the **Mishna**.
- The **Mishna** codifies Jewish belief and practice roughly **200–250 years after Christ**; most of it reflects earlier practice, but **care** is needed when projecting every detail back to the first century.
- The **Corban** rule appears in Aboth; the claim is that it belongs to **oral Torah** revealed through Moses and the rabbis—**parallel in structure**, White argues, to claims that **bishops** preserve **apostolic oral tradition** outside Scripture.

### Critique of Rome’s use of “living tradition”

- White contends that **functional** Roman Catholic teaching treats **tradition** as a **second source** of revelation (whether or not that is the official formulation).
- He says critics are told to “show” the oral tradition, but what is offered tends to be **what Rome has already defined** from tradition—while **new** definitions could appear later with **no independent check** accessible to the layperson.
- He lists **dogmas** he says were **developed** and later defined: **Immaculate Conception** (mid-19th century), **papal infallibility** (First Vatican Council), **Assumption of Mary** (mid-20th century), with varying degrees of **anathema** attached.

### Matthew 23 and the “chair of Moses”

- White situates **Matthew 23** in a **judgment** context against **hypocritical leaders**.
- **“Chair of Moses”**: White describes **archaeological/synagogue** background: a **seat** near the **scroll storage**, from which **Scripture was read**—**Moses** as the **origin of the written oracles**, not a proof-text for an **undocumented oral magisterium**.
- He argues the command to **do what they say** (when they read Moses) **contrasts their teaching from the Scriptures** with **their deeds**—not an endorsement of **extra-scriptural revelation**.
- He alleges **bias** when apologists cite **deuterocanonical** background for some points while **those who “sat” in Jewish teaching authority** did not treat those books as **canonical** in the same way—he frames this as inconsistency.

### Canon and Trent (asides in the audio)

- White mentions **April 1546** and the **Council of Trent** on the **canon** of Scripture.
- He cites **Pope Gregory the Great** (per his claim) on **2 Maccabees** not being canonical scripture, and contrasts with later **dogmatic** definition—used to illustrate **development** and disputed historical claims.

### 2 Thessalonians 2:15 and 3:6 — White’s exegesis

- **2:15** “traditions” **plural**, taught to the **whole church** at Thessalonica—“**you**” plural, not a secret deposit for bishops only.
- **“By word” vs “by letter”**: White argues **logos** refers to **Paul’s preaching while present**; **epistle** refers to **written documents**—already including **1 Thessalonians** (he notes **2 Thessalonians** presupposes prior letters).
- Therefore, he concludes, the passage is about the **gospel already delivered** to that congregation, not a warrant for **centuries-later** dogmas unknown to the first-century church.
- **3:6** “tradition which you received from us” is read in **immediate context**: discipline against **unruly** persons disturbing the church (e.g. **eschatological confusion** and **idleness** in the Thessalonian situation), not a proof for **Marian** dogmas.

### Methodological asides

- Story of **checking Matatics’s cassette** reference-by-reference: confidence and rhetoric vs **context**.
- Reference to **Carl Keating** / **Patrick Madrid** debates vs fundamentalists in **Denver**—White’s point: **fundamentalism** often lacked **presuppositional** engagement with Catholic arguments.
- Tone: White says he has **refuted Staples** on these texts **many times** across media; encourages listeners to **slow down**, read **context**, and not confuse **rhetorical flourish** with **exegetical proof**.

## Core Ideas

- **Sola scriptura** in this lecture is framed as **Scripture’s supremacy as the test** for traditions claimed to be divine—not as “all tradition is evil.”
- **Matthew 15 / Mark 7** is used to show **oral Torah claims** (Corban) **measured by written Torah**.
- **Catholic apologetics** often cites **2 Thessalonians 2:15** to justify **oral apostolic tradition** distinct from Scripture; White argues the **pastoral and epistolary context** limits the referent.
- **Chair of Moses** is interpreted as **synagogue Scripture-reading office**, not **proof of oral revelation parallel to Rome’s magisterium**.
- **Development of doctrine** (Newman) is portrayed as functionally allowing **new revelatory content** over time, which White rejects as matching **apostolic** delivery in Thessalonians.

## Expected Outcome

After taking notes from this outline and watching or listening to the source material, an **A+ student** would be capable of:

### Knowing

- Trace White’s argument that **Matthew 15 / Mark 7** pits **Jesus’s appeal to written Scripture** against a **Pharisaic tradition** (**Corban**) claimed as **divine oral Torah**.
- Explain how **tractate Aboth** and the **Mishna’s** date affect using later rabbinic texts as **first-century** background.
- Describe White’s **chair of Moses** reading: **seat for reading Moses’s scrolls** in synagogue worship vs a **magisterial** office proving **extra-biblical revelation**.
- Summarize White’s view of **2 Thessalonians 2:15**: **traditions** already delivered to **all** Thessalonian believers via **Paul’s preaching** and **prior epistles** (e.g. **1 Thessalonians**).
- Connect **3:6** to **congregational discipline** and **specific Thessalonian problems**, not to **undefined later dogmas**.
- Name the **Marian and papal dogmas** White uses as examples of **tradition-based** definitions over time.
- Recognize the **Jerry Matatics cassette** anecdote as a methodological warning: **verify citations** in **context**.
- Note White’s aside on **Trent**, **canon**, and **patristic/medieval** opinions on **deuterocanonical** books as part of his **Roman Catholic development** critique.

### Reciting

- Give a **one-minute** version: **Corban** as claimed **oral Torah** → **Jesus tests by Scripture** → **2 Thessalonians** about **apostolic delivery to Thessalonica**, not **secret oral magisterium** → **chair of Moses** about **reading Scripture** in synagogue.

### Sharing

- Walk another student through **why** White says **“not all tradition is condemned”** does not answer **how** to **verify** **divine** tradition without **Scripture’s supremacy** in the **Matthew 15** logic.
- Explain to a peer how **plural “traditions”** in **2:15** functions in White’s reading versus a **modern Catholic** **oral-tradition** apologetic.

### Conversing

- Debate whether **Matthew 23:1–3** is better read as **White** presents (Scripture-reading seat + hypocrisy judgment) or as **Staples-style** **legitimate extrabiblical tradition**.
- Compare **Jewish oral Torah claims** around **Corban** with **Catholic claims** about **apostolic succession** and **sacred tradition**: where the analogies hold or break down.
- Discuss whether **2 Thessalonians 2:15** must refer only to **eventually written** apostolic teaching or can **legitimately** extend to **later church-defined** tradition.
