# Summary

James White responds to Tim Staples’s Catholic Answers Live exchange with a caller about **sola scriptura** and **tradition**. White argues that **Matthew 15 / Mark 7** does not merely contrast “bad” tradition with “good” tradition in the abstract, but shows **Jesus testing a tradition the Pharisees treated as divine oral Torah (Corban)** by the **written word of God**. He ties the **Mishna** (notably **Aboth**) to the claim that the Corban rule belonged to **oral Torah**, and draws a structural parallel to **Roman Catholic** claims about **bishops** and **oral apostolic tradition**. He then reinterprets **Matthew 23**’s **chair of Moses** as the **synagogue seat for reading Scripture**, set within a **judgment** context, and exegetes **2 Thessalonians 2:15** and **3:6** as referring to **Paul’s preaching and letters** to the **whole Thessalonian church**, with **3:6** addressing **congregational disorder**, not undocumented later dogmas. The talk includes methodological warnings (checking proof-texts, e.g. the **Jerry Matatics** cassette story) and asides on **canon**, **Trent**, and **doctrinal development** (e.g. **Newman**).

# Core Question

When religious leaders claim **divine tradition** (whether **oral Torah** or **apostolic succession**), **what criterion** does the New Testament provide to distinguish **true** from **false**—and do **2 Thessalonians 2:15** and **Matthew 23** support a **magisterial oral tradition** parallel to **Rome’s teaching office**, as Catholic apologists often argue?

# Key Concepts

- **Sola scriptura** (in White’s framing): **Scripture as the supreme test** for traditions claimed to be from God, not denial of every use of “tradition.”
- **Oral Torah** and **Corban**: a **claimed** Mosaic line of tradition **tested by** written commandments.
- **Mishna / Aboth**: later codification; **care** in retrojecting every detail to Jesus’s day.
- **Chair of Moses**: **archaeological-liturgical** reading (reading **scrolls**) versus **magisterium** reading.
- **2 Thessalonians 2:15**: **“Word” vs “letter”** as **Paul’s presence** vs **epistles** (e.g. **1 Thessalonians**), addressed to **all** believers in the church.
- **Development of doctrine** and **post-apostolic definitions** (Marian and papal dogmas) as **pressure points** in White’s argument.

# Why It Matters

The talk is a **presuppositional and exegetical** primer for **Protestant–Catholic** dialogue: it tries to show **how** rapid-fire citations can **sound** persuasive until **context** and **background sources** are checked, and it frames **Scripture vs tradition** debates as **questions of authority and verification**, not only vocabulary.
