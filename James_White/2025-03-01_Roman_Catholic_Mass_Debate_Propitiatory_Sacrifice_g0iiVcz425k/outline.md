## Detailed Outline

### Setting and resolution

- Hosted at **125 Church** (Reformed Baptist) in **Jonesboro, Arkansas**; moderator **Keith Enck** (elder pastor). Format: 30 minutes of opening speeches (15 each), two rounds of 15-minute cross-examination per debater, 10-minute intermission, 5-minute closings each, then about 30 minutes of audience and online Q&A.
- **Resolution:** Whether **the Mass is a propitiatory sacrifice** (Roman Catholic affirmative; Protestant Reformed Baptist negative).
- **Affirmative:** **Joe Heschmeyer** — staff apologist at **Catholic Answers** (audio/transcript often renders the surname as “Heschm” / “hesm”); former attorney and seminarian (Archdiocese of Kansas City in Kansas); education cited includes Western University, Georgetown Law, **Kenrick-Glennon Seminary**, and the **Angelicum** (Rome).
- **Negative:** **James R. White** — Alpha and Omega Ministries; professor at **Grace Bible Theological Seminary**; pastor/elder at **Apologia Church** (Arizona); debate count cited as **195th** public debate.

### Joe Heschmeyer — affirmative opening (six lines of argument)

1. **Reformers as historical witnesses** — Luther (*Bondage of the Will*) and Calvin are quoted as complaining that belief in the Mass as a **good work and sacrifice** / **sacrifice and oblation for remission of sins** was widespread; Heschm argues this shows **pre-Reformation consensus** rather than a marginal Catholic opinion.
2. **Old Testament** — **Isaiah 66:21**: God will take **some** Gentiles as **priests and Levites**; a priest **offers sacrifice** (appeal to **Hebrews 5**). **Malachi 1:7–14**: corrupt priests profane **the Lord’s table**; yet among the nations a **pure offering** and incense from **rising to setting of the sun** — read as **global, sacrificial** New-Covenant worship (early Christian use of Malachi 1 for the Eucharist is previewed).
3. **New Testament** — **1 Corinthians 10:16–22**: cup of blessing / bread as **participation (koinōnia)** in Christ’s blood and body; **one loaf**; comparison to **Israel eating sacrifices** and to **pagan altars**; warning against **Lord’s cup** and **demons’ cup** — argued to require treating the Eucharist as **altar/sacrifice** language, parallel to Malachi’s “table of the Lord.”
4. **Pre–AD 200 witnesses** — **Didache**: Lord’s Day assembly, Eucharist after reconciliation so **“your sacrifice be not defiled”**; **Justin Martyr** ties Malachi 1 to the Church’s **named** Eucharistic offering; **Irenaeus** on Malachi and **worship in spirit and truth** (**John 4**).
5. **Logic of sacrifice (Passover pattern)** — Slaughter is not the whole rite; **eating** applies the sacrifice (Passover on **15 Nisan** after slaughter on **14th**). **“Do this in memory of me”** — **anamnesis** tied to **memorial sacrifice** language (only other NT use in **Hebrews**, per Heschm). **Once-for-all** death, **ongoing** covenantal participation by eating.
6. **Nature of worship** — Distinction: **study** (about God), **prayer** (to God), **worship** (offering **to** God). Synagogue in **Luke** has reading/preaching but is **not** called a house of prayer/worship like the temple; **Everett Ferguson** cited on sacrifice as **universal language of worship** in antiquity.

### James White — negative opening

- Reads **Roman Catholic magisterial** language: priests celebrate **Divine worship** especially the **Eucharistic sacrifice**; **true and proper sacrifice** offered **in persona Christi** as **alter Christus**; **propitiatory sacrifice for living and the dead**; Church **perpetuates the sacrifice of the Cross throughout the ages** (transcript cites **“December 7th, 1865”** and **“Pope Paul v 6”** — audio aligns with **Second Vatican Council**, ***Presbyterorum Ordinis*** **7 December 1965**, **Paul VI**).
- **Propitiatory** is the controlling term — not merely “sacrifice” in the sense of **praise** or **spiritual sacrifices** (**Romans 12**).
- Contrasts **finished** cross work (**Hebrews 7–10**) with **perpetuated** sacramental re-presentation: **transubstantiation** and **Aristotelian** substance/accidents categories as later explanatory scaffolding; primitive church had **real presence** ideas, not fully **Tridentine** categories, in White’s telling.
- **Pastoral/systemic consequence:** Mass as propitiation yet **imperfecting** — many Mass-goers still face **purgatory**, **masses for the dead**, possible **mortal sin** and loss; cites **Ludwig Ott** (*Fundamentals of Catholic Dogma*) on **uncertainty of the state of grace** vs **Romans 5:1** (**peace with God**).
- **Hebrews exegesis (rapid survey):** **7:24–25** permanent priesthood, **always lives to make intercession**; **8** New Covenant — sins **remembered no more**; **9:11–12, 24–28** — entered **once for all**, **not** repeatedly; **10:1–4** repetitive sacrifices = **reminder of sins**; **10:10, 14** **one offering** sanctifies and **perfects** the sanctified; **10:3** **anamnesis** of sins yearly vs Supper as **remembrance of the sin-bearer**, not a propitiatory re-offering.

### Cross-examination — White examines Heschm (round 1)

- **Sacramental priesthood** required for a valid Catholic Mass — Heschm agrees a **non-ordained** person cannot confect it.
- Debate over **presbyter / episkopos** identity vs separate **sacerdotal** office; **1 Timothy / Titus** qualifications; whether **hiereus** (“priest”) must appear for a **sacrificial** priesthood.
- **Ignatius of Antioch (~107)** cited by Heschm for **bishop/presbyter** distinction and **altar** language; White later questions **Ignatian authenticity** (textual tradition) in round 2 context.
- **Hebrews “once for all”** — Heschm: much of Hebrews’ “offering” language concerns **Ascension/heavenly presentation**, not only Good Friday; White: **offering** completed on **Calvary**, **presentation** of finished work; repetition on earth still does not **perfect**.
- **Mortal/venial** sin and **1 John 5** — disputed reading; **Jerome Biblical Commentary** (forward by **Pope Francis**) briefly argued over as authority.
- **Interpretation** — appeal to **Keith Mathison** (*The Shape of Sola Scriptura*): no “raw” scripture, only interpreted scripture; discussion of whether Scripture’s meaning is **objective** yet **access** is fallible; exchange on **“untaught and unstable”** (**2 Peter 3**) and logical inference; White calls time.

### Cross-examination — Heschm examines White (round 1)

- **Good Friday vs Ascension** — White affirms sacrificial **completion** on the cross with **heavenly presentation** as one work; **Lamb standing as though slain** (**Revelation 5**) as completed sacrifice visibly represented.
- **Hebrews 9:24–25** — contrast yearly high priest with Christ **not** offering Himself **often**; Heschm presses **Yom Kippur** pattern (blood application distinct from slaughter); White distinguishes **types** vs Christ’s **single** oblation.
- **Hebrews 7:26** “exalted above the heavens” — timing debate.
- **Hebrews 9:18–22** covenant ratification by **blood**; bridge to **Last Supper** “**blood of the covenant**” — Heschm parallels **Exodus 24** (blood on people and altar; elders **see God**, eat and drink); White: **Passover typology**, certainty of cross to come, **not** transubstantiation at the Supper; disciples could not yet fully understand.

### Cross-examination — White examines Heschm (round 2)

- **John O’Brien**, *The Faith of Millions* — dramatic “bring Christ down” language; Heschm: **poetic** priesthood section vs Eucharistic chapter; still affirms **propitiatory** language in O’Brien with **nuance** on **local presence in heaven** vs Eucharistic presence.
- **Eastern Orthodoxy** — **Michael Pomazanski** (Russian Orthodox) cited on Eucharist as **propitiatory** for living and dead; discussion of **blood** requirement (**Hebrews 9:22**) vs **bloodless** offering as **same** sacrifice presented.
- **Gregory Dix**, *The Shape of the Liturgy* — patristic liturgical continuity; debate over whether **sacrificial language** in early fathers equals **Tridentine propitiation** for living/dead in modern Roman sense.
- **Malachi 1** — White: incense/grain offering texts are **not** propitiatory like **blooded** sin offerings; Heschm: propitiation tied to **Christ’s blood** fulfilling type, Malachi as partial prophecy.
- **Isaiah 66** — White reads fuller context: **grain offering** procession of nations; questions **NT authors’** direct use of Isaiah 66 for Heschm’s structure.

### Cross-examination — Heschm examines White (round 2)

- **Luther**, *The Abomination of the Secret Mass* — violent rhetoric against the Mass; White: affirm **damnable error** in the **teaching**; pastoral nuance on Catholics whose **trust** may exceed their **theology**; struggles with those who **once affirmed** Reformed doctrine then moved to Catholic propitiatory Mass.
- **Visible church / global apostasy** — Calvin’s **Institutes** “no salvation outside the visible church” vs persecution/repression narratives; **Fulgentius**, **Augustine** mentioned; White: **doctrinal development “wrong direction”** yet some believers held mixed errors.
- **Cyril of Jerusalem** (Catechetical Lecture 23) quoted on **Spirit** upon gifts, **bloodless service** completed “**over that sacrifice of propitiation**” — debate over **anachronistic** reading vs **real presence** without full **Aristotle–Aquinas** apparatus.
- **Fourth Lateran (1215)** and **transubstantiation** terminology; **Aquinas** and **Trent** as intellectual backbone; Eucharistic miracles / **physicality** debates.
- **Ignatius** “medicine of immortality” — White: anti-**docetic** context (**flesh and blood**), not automatic proof of **propitiatory** Mass theology.

### Closings

- **White** — **Sola Scriptura**; no **NT sacerdotal** priesthood qualifications; **Hebrews**’s **perfection** vs Roman **loss of justification**, **purgatorial suffering** (**satisfactio poenalis**); weekly Supper as **non-propitiatory remembrance**; warning about **reading later dogma** into early sources (analogy to **Marian** development).
- **Heschm** — Not only “Rome says so”; **Anglican** liturgical scholarship on **pre-Nicene** unanimity on sacrificial Eucharist; **Cyril of Jerusalem** (~348–350) explicit “**propitiation for the living and the dead**”; trust **line of early Christians** vs lone modern reader; **Hebrews** and **Passover** logic imply **post-Calvary** presentation and **eating**; invitation to **Mass** locally.

### Q&A highlights (themes only)

- **Hebrews 9:22** vs **“same sacrifice”** language in Catholic catechism — bloodless as **one** sacrifice’s **application**, not a **new** shedding.
- **Grace as commodity** / **temporal punishment** vs **Hebrews 7:25** — Heschm: marital reconciliation analogy, **1 Corinthians 11** discipline; White: **indulgences** and **treasury of merit**.
- **Canon** and “consensus” — White: **theological** canonicity vs using consensus to fix **eucharistic doctrine**; Heschm: cannot reject **Matthean** consensus arbitrarily.
- **OT priesthood ethnicity** — Heschm: **Isaiah 66** Gentile priests; **Melchizedek** pattern; White: all believers **priests**, no **propitiatory** offerings by a **sacramental** caste.
- **Trent** “daily immolated” — Heschm: **unbloody** representation, not **re-crucifixion**; White: repetition and **Hebrews 10** logic.
- **John 6**, **imputation vs infusion**, **Hebrews 6/10** warnings, apostasy, **McGrath** on novelty — brief exchanges restating core positions.

## Core Ideas

- The debate is not “is there sacrifice language?” but whether the Mass is **propitiatory** in the sense Rome teaches: **true and proper sacrifice** applied to **living and dead** through **ministerial priesthood** and the Eucharist.
- **Hebrews 7–10** is the Protestant backbone: **one offering**, **perfection**, **seated** priest, **no repeated** oblation as under the Levitical shadow.
- Catholic argument weaves **OT prophecy**, **Pauline altar/table language**, **early liturgical texts**, and **sacrificial phenomenology** (Passover eaten) into a **participation** model, not a “second crucifixion.”
- **Hermeneutics and history** — whose reading of **Hebrews** and whose **patristic/liturgical** sources authorize conclusions?
