# Summary

Joshua Schooping continues his evangelical critique of Eastern Orthodox Mariology by rehearsing **typical apologetic rejoinders** (denial of “soft selling,” genre/poetry, hyperbole, alleged contradictions, euphemism) and arguing each fails to defuse the **plain force** of Marian prayer language. He then walks through **standard prayer-book texts** (including a pre-communion prayer attributed to **Chrysostom**) and the **Akathist to the Theotokos**, claiming they repeatedly treat Mary as **soteriological agent**—**propitiation**, **salvation**, **freedom of approach**, **deliverance from evil**, and similar—thereby **confusing** her role with **Christ’s**. He closes by urging **Scripture** (2 Timothy 3:16–17) and pastoral concern for Orthodox hearers.

# Core Question

When Marian hymnody uses **Christ-shaped titles** and **exclusive** intercessory language, can appeals to **poetry**, **hyperbole**, or **adjacent Trinitarian prayers** adequately preserve **Christ alone** as **mediator** and **propitiation**, or do they instead **form** believers in a **different spiritual imagination** than the gospel the speaker defends?

# Key Concepts

- **Soft selling**: withholding upfront how **icon veneration** and certain **conciliar** claims frame **outsiders** and **discipline**.
- **Deception vs lying**: passing on **false belief** without **intent** to deceive.
- **Confusion vs contradiction**: the problem is **Mary invested with divine/soteriological properties**, not merely **logical clash**.
- **“Through thee” / maternal causality**: crediting Mary with outcomes **proper to Christ’s** work (speaker’s **reductio** with ancestry).
- **Akathist** as **canonical popular devotion**: **liturgical formation** vs **apologetic nuance**.
- **Sufficiency of Scripture**: **2 Timothy 3:16–17** as the speaker’s anchor.

# Why It Matters

For **Protestant–Orthodox** conversation, the talk names **recurring rhetorical moves** around **Mary** and ties them to **actual liturgical texts** many believers pray from memory—so listeners can see **why** each side may **talk past** the other on **idolatry**, **honor**, and **the gospel**. For **pastoral** and **intellectual** purposes, it also raises how **worship language** **trains** intuition about **who saves** and **how** God is **approached**, which matters well beyond **single proof-text** exchanges.
