# Summary

Pastor Joshua Schooping argues that Eastern Orthodox defenses of icons often fix attention on the **difference between adoration and veneration**, but that debate is a **distraction** from a sharper issue: **Seventh Ecumenical Council** language (as he quotes from *NPNF* 2nd series, vol. 14) treats **saluting and venerating** holy images as **necessary**, not optional, with **anathemas** for refusal. He walks through the council’s own gloss on **proskynēsis**—linking it to **kissing**, **salutation**, and **strong affection** for the **icon**—and then cites **conciliar** and **canon-law handbook** definitions of **anathema** to insist the stakes are **eternal**, not rhetorical. **C. F. W. Walther** is brought in to frame **salvation tied to visible church membership** as a **law/gospel** mistake. The lecture closes with **Revelation** and **Acts** episodes where **angels** and **Peter** **refuse proskynēsis**, even when no one confuses them with God—then asks how **mandatory veneration of their images** can cohere with those texts if veneration is a **salvation condition**.

# Core Question

Is the standard **adoration vs. veneration** distinction the real crux of the icon dispute, or does **binding conciliar veneration**—with **damnatory anathemas** and an expansive list of **honored images**—reframe the argument so that vocabulary distinctions are **secondary** to **soteriology** and **Scripture’s treatment of creaturely honor**?

# Key Concepts

- **Red herring**: the adoration/veneration vocabulary fight vs. **necessary** icon veneration under **anathema**
- **Proskynēsis** (council’s etymology): **kiss**, **greeting**, **strong love** toward what is reverenced
- **Conciliarity** and **immutable dogmatic anathemas** vs. **economia** in discipline
- **Anathema** as **outer darkness**, **Gehenna**, **separation from God**, and **loss of salvation** (sources cited)
- **Walther** on **law/gospel** and **visible-church** conditions for salvation
- **Particularist ecclesiology** vs. **invisible church** (as argued in the lecture)
- **NT refusal of creaturely proskynēsis** (Revelation, Acts) vs. **required** veneration of **saints’/angels’** images

# Why It Matters

The talk tries to move interfaith argument from **semantic triage** (“it is not *latria*”) to **stakes and scope**: what councils claim is **obligatory**, what **anathema** is understood to mean, and whether **New Testament** precedents about **honor toward creatures** fit comfortably with **soteriological** veneration of **their images**. For students of **Orthodox–Protestant** dialogue, it is a compressed case study in how **primary sources**, **canon law handbooks**, and **confessional** theology are woven into one **critical** line of reasoning.
